- [ ] Leadership understands SPAR-Kit is augmentation, not automation
- [ ] Clear communication plan for employees

- [ ] Feedback mechanisms established

- [ ] Backup procedures for session data

## üó£Ô∏è Communication Guidelines

### What to Tell Employees

**DO Say:**
- "SPAR-Kit helps you explore decisions more thoroughly"
- "It surfaces perspectives you might not have considered"
- "You remain the decision-maker"
- "The tool is designed to enhance your judgment, not replace it"

**DON'T Say:**
- "SPAR-Kit will make decisions for you"
- "We're implementing AI to increase efficiency"
- "This will reduce headcount"
- "Trust the AI recommendation"

## üìä Deployment Phases

### Phase 1: Pilot (Weeks 1-4)

- **Scope**: 5-10 volunteer users
- **Decisions**: Low-stakes, reversible
- **Support**: High-touch, daily check-ins
- **Goal**: Gather feedback, identify issues

### Phase 2: Expansion (Weeks 5-12)

- **Scope**: Full team or department
- **Decisions**: Standard operational decisions
- **Support**: Weekly office hours, Slack channel
- **Goal**: Establish habits, refine processes

### Phase 3: Scale (Month 3+)

- **Scope**: Organization-wide (where appropriate)
- **Decisions**: All appropriate decision types
- **Support**: Self-service + documentation
- **Goal**: Embedded in decision culture

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Red Lines: Never Deploy For

### Prohibited Use Cases

| Use Case | Why Prohibited |
|----------|---------------|
| Performance evaluation | Power imbalance, bias risk |
| Hiring/firing decisions | Legal liability, ethical concerns |
| Surveillance of employees | Privacy violation, trust destruction |
| Automated policy enforcement | Removes human judgment |
| Compensation decisions | Fairness, legal compliance |

### Warning Signs

Stop deployment if you observe:
- Employees feeling surveilled
- Pressure to "trust the AI"
- Resistance to override recommendations
- Focus on speed over quality
- Fear of disagreeing with AI
