## Direction Profiles

### ğŸ”µ NORTH â€” The Visionary

**Element**: Air (expansive, ascending, future-oriented)

**Core Question**: "Where are we going?"

**Focus**: Long-term vision, strategic direction, aspirational goals, ideal future state

**Strength**: Prevents short-term thinking, maintains purpose, connects decisions to meaning

**Shadow**: Can disconnect from reality, ignore constraints, become preachy

**Natural Allies**: Often finds common ground with EAST (both future-focused)

**Natural Tension**: Clashes with WEST (vision vs execution) and SOUTH (future vs past)

**Signature Phrases**:
- "In 10 years, will this decision still make sense?"
- "What's the bigger picture here?"
- "Our true north is..."
- "This is about more than just [immediate concern]"
- "What kind of organization do we want to become?"

**When NORTH is Missing**: Decisions become tactical and reactive. Teams optimize for short-term gains while losing strategic coherence.

**When NORTH Dominates**: Decisions become aspirational fantasies. Teams plan castles in the sky without foundations.

### ğŸŸ  EAST â€” The Challenger

**Element**: Fire (transformative, disruptive, energetic)

**Core Question**: "What's emerging?"

**Focus**: Competition, disruption, paradigm shifts, external forces, emerging threats and opportunities

**Strength**: Prevents complacency, spots threats early, maintains competitive awareness

**Shadow**: Creates anxiety, undervalues stability, prone to trend-chasing

**Natural Allies**: Often aligns with NORTH (both forward-looking)

**Natural Tension**: Clashes with SOUTH (new vs known) and WEST (change vs stability)

**Signature Phrases**:
- "Have you seen what [competitor] is doing?"
- "The market is shifting faster than we think"
- "What if we're disrupted by..."
- "This worked before, but the game has changed"
- "First-mover advantage matters here"

**When EAST is Missing**: Teams become complacent. Disruption arrives as a surprise. Competitors outflank unnoticed.

**When EAST Dominates**: Teams pivot constantly. Every trend triggers a strategy change. Stability becomes impossible.

### ğŸŸ¤ SOUTH â€” The Sage

**Element**: Earth (grounded, historical, evidence-based)

**Core Question**: "What do we know?"

**Focus**: Evidence, history, lessons learned, accumulated wisdom, track record

**Strength**: Prevents reinventing the wheel, grounds decisions in evidence, carries institutional memory

**Shadow**: Can anchor to the past, miss discontinuities, be overly conservative

**Natural Allies**: Often supports WEST (both grounded in reality)

**Natural Tension**: Clashes with EAST (past vs emerging) and NORTH (evidence vs vision)

**Signature Phrases**:
- "Last time we tried this, here's what happened..."
- "The data actually shows..."
- "History teaches us that..."
- "Let's look at the evidence before deciding"
- "What does our track record tell us?"

**When SOUTH is Missing**: Teams repeat past mistakes. Decisions ignore available data. Institutional memory is lost.

**When SOUTH Dominates**: Teams become paralyzed by precedent. "We've always done it this way" blocks innovation. Past success becomes a cage.

### ğŸŸ¢ WEST â€” The Pragmatist

**Element**: Water (adaptive, practical, reality-testing)

**Core Question**: "How will this work?"

**Focus**: Execution, resources, constraints, operational reality, feasibility

**Strength**: Prevents fantasy planning, ensures feasibility, surfaces practical obstacles

**Shadow**: Can limit ambition, focus on obstacles over opportunities, become a blocker

**Natural Allies**: Often partners with SOUTH (both reality-focused)

**Natural Tension**: Clashes with NORTH (execution vs vision) and EAST (stability vs disruption)

**Signature Phrases**:
- "Practically speaking, who's going to do this?"
- "With our current resources..."
- "What's the actual timeline here?"
- "Have we considered the operational impact?"
- "That's a great idea, but let me tell you about the dependencies"

**When WEST is Missing**: Plans become fantasies. Resources are overcommitted. Teams promise what they can't deliver.

**When WEST Dominates**: Ambition dies. Every idea is "too hard." Innovation suffocates under feasibility concerns.

## Using the Directions in SPAR

### Step 1: Present All Four
Always present all four directions, even briefly. Missing a direction creates a blind spot.

### Step 2: Let Them Clash
The value is in the tension, not the agreement. Don't smooth over disagreements.

### Step 3: Synthesize, Don't Choose
The goal is integration, not selecting a "winner." The best decisions hold multiple truths.

### Step 4: Name the Dominant Voice
In any decision, one direction usually dominates the initial framing. Name it.
- "This decision is being led by WEST (feasibility concerns)"
- "The conversation is dominated by EAST (competitive anxiety)"

### Step 5: Amplify the Quiet Voice
Deliberately strengthen the direction that's being underweighted. Often the synthesis lives there.
