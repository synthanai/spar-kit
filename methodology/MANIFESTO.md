## What is SPAR?

**SPAR** is a decision-making methodology that uses structured disagreement between distinct personas to surface insights that consensus-seeking processes systematically miss. Whether those personas are embodied by humans, simulated by AI, or both â€” the methodology remains the same.

| Letter | Meaning | Definition |

## The Core Insight

**The problem isn't AI. The problem is isolated reasoning.**

When you deliberate alone â€” whether you're a lone leader, a homogeneous team, a person chatting with AI, or AI reasoning with itself â€” you get something that *looks* like perspective but isn't. It's variations on a theme, structured to appear balanced while hiding shared assumptions.

| Context | The Problem |
|---------|-------------|
| **Leader alone** | Personal blind spots, confirmation bias, echo chamber of self |
| **Team together** | Groupthink, hierarchy silencing dissent, shared frames |
| **Person + AI** | Single reasoning context producing false "balance" |
| **AI alone** | Sophisticated coherence without genuine tension |

This is why "ask for multiple perspectives" fails. The perspectives don't actually conflict â€” they're generated from the same underlying assumptions.

**SPAR is different.**

By instantiating separate personas with coherent value systems that *genuinely disagree*, SPAR produces:
- Real arguments, not balanced trade-off lists
- Exposed assumptions, not hidden consensus
- Fourth options, not predetermined choices
- Decisions you can stand behind, not sophisticated confirmation

## The GRACE Principles

> **G**round Â· **R**eveal Â· **A**pproach Â· **C**ourt Â· **E**xpose

*Be GRACEful: the stance from which truth can be approached but never owned.*

### G â€” Ground
**Still yourself before synthesis.**

After debate and before action, create space for reflection without agenda. The deepest insights often arrive in the silence after the tension.

*"What surprised us? What are we not ready to decide?"*

### R â€” Reveal
**Surface the biases and anxieties driving the conversation.**

Before turning to AI debates, humans must have the honest conversations they are avoiding. Process should never be an escape route from difficult human relationships.

*"What uncomfortable human conversation are we substituting for?"*

### A â€” Approach
**Seek the adjacent possible, not defended positions.**

At least one participant must steelman an alternative that might transform the situation entirely but feels unrealistic from where you currently stand.

*"What options are we not considering because they feel too far from current reality?"*

### C â€” Court
**Welcome dissent. Invite it. Make space for it.**

Every debate must include a voice explicitly tasked with finding weaknesses in emerging consensus. Not as "devil's advocate theater," but as genuine intellectual opposition.

*"What's the strongest case against what we're about to decide?"*

### E â€” Expose
**Acknowledge what you don't know. Name the missing perspectives.**

Every synthesis must state its own limits: "This was seen from *here*, with *these* distinctions." This maintains the humility necessary for continuous adaptation.

*"What did we NOT consider? What perspectives were missing?"*
